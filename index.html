Pepsi, created in 1893 by Caleb Bradham and originally named "Brad's Drink," is a leading global brand of carbonated soft drinks produced by PepsiCo. Renamed Pepsi-Cola in 1898, it gained popularity by offering larger volumes at competitive prices during the Great Depression. Known for innovative marketing, celebrity endorsements, and memorable ad campaigns, Pepsi has established itself as Coca-Cola's main rival in the "Cola Wars." PepsiCo, formed by merging Pepsi-Cola with Frito-Lay in 1965, has expanded to include a diverse range of beverages and snacks. With a strong global presence and ongoing sustainability efforts, Pepsi remains a prominent cultural and commercial force.
